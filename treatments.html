Our approach

Treatments

A multi-disciplinary approach combining orofacial myofunctional therapy with complementary modalities for comprehensive care.

Our treatment modalities

Primary therapy

Orofacial Myofunctional Therapy (OMT)

OMT re-educates the muscles of the mouth, tongue, and face to restore proper rest posture and function. Rather than masking symptoms, it corrects the underlying dysfunction — and clinical studies show treatment is 80–90% effective, with results maintained long after therapy ends.

The five goals of OMT
1

Eliminate oral habits

Addressing thumb sucking, lip habits, and other patterns — gently and without shame.

2

Establish correct oral rest position

Training the tongue to rest against the palate and the lips to seal comfortably.

3

Establish proper chewing & swallowing

Building new movement patterns that feel natural and become automatic.

4

Re-pattern head & neck posture

Addressing postural patterns that contribute to orofacial dysfunction.

5

Habituation of the new muscle pattern

New patterns become automatic and last years after therapy ends.

Complementary therapy

Craniosacral Therapy (CST)

A gentle, hands-on treatment that uses light touch to release restrictions in the craniosacral system. For OMT patients, CST helps release tension in the jaw, face, and neck that contributes to improper oral posture. Safe for all ages, from infants to adults.

How it helps OMT patients

Releases restrictions in the head and face that may limit tongue mobility, jaw movement, or nasal breathing — accelerating OMT outcomes.

Common applications

Jaw pain and TMJ dysfunction, chronic headaches, tongue-tie release support, and infant feeding difficulties.

Complementary therapy

Primitive Reflex Integration

Primitive reflexes are automatic movement patterns from infancy that should naturally become inactive as the brain matures. When retained, they can keep the tongue low and forward, promote mouth breathing, and make it difficult to establish the new patterns OMT teaches.

How integration works

Targeted movement exercises — simple enough to do at home — progressively train the nervous system to release immature patterns and allow mature motor control to take over.

Signs of retained reflexes

Poor coordination, difficulty sitting still, sensory sensitivities, persistent tongue thrust, open-mouth posture, and difficulty transitioning to mature swallowing patterns.

Complementary therapy

Buteyko Breathing

A breathing retraining method that restores functional nasal breathing. Mouth breathing drops the tongue from the palate, alters facial growth, and reinforces the dysfunction OMT aims to correct. Buteyko breaks this cycle at its source.

Why nasal breathing matters

Nasal breathing allows the tongue to rest against the palate, maintaining arch shape and supporting healthy facial development. It also filters, warms, and humidifies air before it reaches the lungs.

Benefits beyond the mouth

Improved sleep, reduced snoring, decreased anxiety, better exercise tolerance, and fewer asthma symptoms — in addition to orofacial benefits.
