Credentials

A career devoted to a single specialty

RDH COM IAOM Certified CST Trained KS Licensed since 1980

Kathleen M. Elder has built an extraordinary career at the intersection of dental hygiene and orofacial myofunctional therapy. A licensed Registered Dental Hygienist in Kansas since 1980, she has practiced in general, periodontal, and orthodontic clinical settings — giving her a uniquely comprehensive understanding of how the mouth, teeth, and surrounding musculature interact.

In 2003, she transitioned to private practice as an orofacial myofunctional therapist — and has worked exclusively in this specialty ever since. In 2008, she attained her Certified Orofacial Myologist (COM) designation through the International Association of Orofacial Myology (IAOM), becoming the first person in the State of Kansas and the Greater Kansas City area to hold this distinction.

She also serves on the dental hygiene team at the KU Medical Center Cleft Palate and Cerebral Palsy Clinics, and has extended her studies to include Craniosacral Therapy (CST).

About the practice name

Why "Digits & Spots"?

The name is a deliberate and affectionate play on the two core concerns the practice treats. "Digits" refers to thumb and finger sucking — digit habits that disrupt oral development. "Spots" refers to tongue position — helping the tongue find its proper "spot" at rest against the palate. Together, they capture the heart of what Kathleen does every day.
