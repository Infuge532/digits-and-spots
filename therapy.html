How treatment works

Orofacial Myofunctional Therapy (OMT)

OMT is rest posture therapy — re-educating the muscles of the mouth, tongue, and face to function the way they were designed to.

80–90%
Treatment effectiveness rate in clinical studies
All ages
Children through adults can benefit from OMT
Long-lasting
Corrections maintained years after therapy ends
What OMT does

Restoring normal posture — for life

Restoring a normal rest posture of the tongue and lips and eliminating tongue thrust can guide teeth into a more desirable relationship during growth years, help orthodontists align teeth and jaws properly, stabilize teeth during and after orthodontic treatment, and enhance overall facial appearance.

For children

Re-establish a normal oral environment in which healthy dental eruption can occur — ideally before or alongside orthodontic treatment.

For adults

Normalize oral postures and functions to create stability in the dental arches — including after orthodontic relapse or surgery.

The five goals of OMT

A clear, step-by-step approach

1

Eliminate oral habits

Addressing thumb sucking, lip habits, and other patterns driving the dysfunction — gently and without shame or punishment.

2

Establish correct oral rest position

Training the tongue to rest properly against the palate, and the lips to seal comfortably without strain.

3

Establish proper chewing & swallowing

Building new movement patterns for eating and swallowing that feel natural and become automatic over time.

4

Re-pattern head & neck posture

Addressing related postural patterns in the head and neck that contribute to or result from orofacial dysfunction.

5

Habituation of the new muscle pattern

The new patterns become automatic — and corrections last years after therapy ends with continued awareness and monitoring.

Therapy methods

Personalized, practical homework

Therapy includes education about proper tongue and lip postures, awareness training for appropriate resting positions, exercises to establish correct swallow movement, and individually designed homework assignments to reinforce what's practiced in session.
