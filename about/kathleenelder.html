About Kathleen

Kathleen Elder

Kathleen Elder is an orofacial myologist that supplements her practice with craniosacral, primitive reflex and Buteyko breathing therapies. She was the first orofacial myologist to be certified by the International Association of Orofacial Myology in the state of Kansas.

Credentials

A career devoted to orofacial health

RDH COM IAOM Certified CST Trained KS Licensed since 1980

Kathleen M. Elder has built an extraordinary career at the intersection of dental hygiene and orofacial myofunctional therapy. A licensed Registered Dental Hygienist in Kansas since 1980, she has practiced in general, periodontal, and orthodontic clinical settings — giving her a uniquely comprehensive understanding of how the mouth, teeth, and surrounding musculature interact.

In 2003, she transitioned to private practice as an orofacial myofunctional therapist — and has worked exclusively in this specialty ever since. In 2008, she attained her Certified Orofacial Myologist (COM) designation through the International Association of Orofacial Myology (IAOM), becoming the first person in the State of Kansas and the Greater Kansas City area to hold this distinction.

Complementary therapies

A multi-disciplinary approach

Beyond orofacial myofunctional therapy, Kathleen supplements her practice with several complementary modalities to provide holistic care:

Craniosacral Therapy (CST)

A gentle, hands-on approach that addresses restrictions in the craniosacral system to improve the functioning of the central nervous system.

Primitive Reflex Integration

Addressing retained primitive reflexes that can interfere with proper orofacial development and function in both children and adults.

Buteyko Breathing

A breathing technique that helps restore nasal breathing, which is essential for proper tongue posture and overall orofacial health.
