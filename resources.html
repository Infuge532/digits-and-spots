Insurance & payment

Insurance & billing information

Orofacial myofunctional therapy is not universally covered by all insurance plans, but many patients do receive partial or full reimbursement. We recommend contacting your insurance provider to ask about coverage for orofacial myofunctional therapy (CPT codes may fall under speech therapy or dental benefit categories).

Medical insurance

Some medical plans cover OMT when prescribed by a physician or dentist. We can provide documentation and superbills to help you file claims with your carrier.

Payment options

We accept cash, check, and major credit cards. Payment is due at the time of service. We are happy to discuss payment arrangements for extended treatment plans.

HSA & FSA accounts

Orofacial myofunctional therapy may be eligible for reimbursement through your Health Savings Account (HSA) or Flexible Spending Account (FSA). Check with your plan administrator.

Questions about coverage?

Call us at 913-206-4016 and we'll help you understand your options and navigate the insurance process before your first visit.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

How long does treatment take?

Treatment length varies by individual, but most therapy programs run between 6–12 months with regular sessions and daily at-home exercises. Kathleen will give you a clear timeline during your evaluation.

What ages do you treat?

We work with patients of all ages — from young children (typically age 4 and up) through adults. It's never too late to address orofacial myofunctional issues.

Do I need a referral?

No referral is required. Many patients come to us directly, though we also work closely with orthodontists, dentists, ENTs, and speech therapists who refer patients to our practice.

Will my child need to miss school?

We offer after-school, evening, and Saturday appointments specifically to minimize disruption to school and work schedules.

Is OMT the same as speech therapy?

No — while there can be overlap, OMT focuses specifically on the resting posture and function of the tongue, lips, and facial muscles. Speech therapy addresses sound production. The two are complementary.

How effective is treatment?

Clinical studies show that orofacial myofunctional therapy is 80–90% effective in correcting swallowing and rest posture functions, with results maintained long after therapy ends.

Professional organizations & resources

These organizations provide additional information about orofacial myofunctional therapy, research, and finding certified practitioners.

International Association of Orofacial Myology (IAOM)

The leading professional organization for orofacial myologists worldwide. IAOM provides certification (COM designation), research, and continuing education. Kathleen received her COM certification through IAOM in 2008.

Academy of Orofacial Myofunctional Therapy (AOMT)

An organization dedicated to advancing the practice and public awareness of orofacial myofunctional therapy through education, research, and professional development.

American Academy of Physiological Medicine & Dentistry (AAPMD)

A multidisciplinary organization focused on the connection between oral health and systemic health, including airway, breathing, and orofacial development.

Buteyko Breathing Educators Association

Resources on Buteyko breathing techniques used to restore healthy nasal breathing patterns — one of the complementary therapies Kathleen incorporates into her practice.
